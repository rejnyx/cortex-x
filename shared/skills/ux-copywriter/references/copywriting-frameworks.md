# Conversion copywriting frameworks — anatomy + hero formulas + CTA bank

> Companion to [`../SKILL.md`](../SKILL.md). Loaded on demand when generating hero copy / landing-page sequences / slogans. Grounded in May 2026 deep research: Wiebe (CopyHackers), Dunford (Positioning), Miller (StoryBrand 2.0), Ulwick (JTBD), Bob Stone's Gem, Shleyner (VeryGoodCopy), Dry (Demand Curve), CXL + GoodUI A/B archives, PLoS ONE first-person-effect meta-analysis.

## 1 — The 10 active frameworks (when each wins)

### AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action
- **Audience**: cold, unaware.
- **Wins**: B2C e-commerce, social ads, impulse purchase.
- **Why**: pattern interrupt that turns 0 attention into a click in 4 short beats.
- **Loses at**: sophisticated B2B — reads as pushy.

### PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solve
- **Audience**: problem-aware, evaluating solutions.
- **Wins**: B2B SaaS, B2B services. The dominant SaaS engine for a reason.
- **Why**: loss aversion. Agitate quantifies the pain in real units (hours, $, deals lost), then the Solve lands as relief.
- **Key**: the Agitate step is where amateurs flinch. Don't. Real numbers, real cost.

### FAB — Features, Advantages, Benefits
- **Audience**: solution-aware, comparison-shopping technical specs.
- **Wins**: dev-tools, prosumer hardware, technical platforms.
- **Why**: skeptical buyers demand hard data before any marketing claim.
- **Pattern**: `[Feature] means [Advantage], so you can [Benefit]`.

### BAB — Before, After, Bridge
- **Audience**: desire-aware (knows the outcome they want, hasn't found the path).
- **Wins**: infoproducts, courses, transformative agency services, fitness.
- **Why**: contrast-based, low pressure, paints the path as hopeful — not painful.

### PPPP — Picture, Promise, Prove, Push
- **Audience**: mixed — long-form sales pages, VSL.
- **Wins**: high-ticket info, complex purchases, long consideration cycles.
- **Why**: 50%+ of the page is `Prove` — matches reality that skeptical buyers want overwhelming proof before they convert.

### Bob Stone's Gem (7-step formula)
- **Audience**: product-aware, ready to act.
- **Wins**: direct-response sales emails, full sales pages, last-mile conversion.
- **The 7 steps**: (1) start with strongest benefit · (2) expand it · (3) tell them exactly what they get · (4) prove it with evidence · (5) name the loss of inaction · (6) summarize benefits · (7) call to action.

### JTBD-for-messaging (Ulwick / Christensen)
- **Audience**: any — but works best in crowded markets where category lines blur.
- **Wins**: innovative platforms, marketplaces, anything that wants to redefine its category.
- **Pattern**: `Hire [product] to [progress the customer wants], so [outcome]`.

### StoryBrand SB7 2.0 (Donald Miller, 2025)
- **Audience**: any, especially when the brand risks sounding self-absorbed.
- **Wins**: B2B + B2C with complex offers, professional services, healthcare.
- **The 7 beats**: hero (customer) → has a problem → meets a guide (brand) → who gives a plan → calls them to action → that avoids failure → and ends in success.
- **Key**: brand is **never** the hero. Customer is the hero. Brand is the wise guide.

### Conversion Copywriting / Copyselling (Joanna Wiebe, 2025/2026)
- **Audience**: any — but requires real Voice-of-Customer data.
- **Wins**: any product willing to invest in customer research before writing.
- **Method**: extract verbatim from reviews + interviews, build a Message Matrix, write directly from customer words. No brainstorming.

### Positioning Method (April Dunford)
- **Audience**: any — used at the very top of the page to anchor the category.
- **Wins**: any product where customers don't yet know what category to compare you against.
- **Pattern**: `Unlike [competitive alternative], [product] is [category] for [audience] that [outcome that matters most]`.

## 2 — Hybridization in production (2026)

Strict framework purity is a junior signal. Production hybrid stack:

| Section | Framework |
|---|---|
| Hero (H1 + subhead) | Dunford positioning to anchor category |
| Problem framing block | PAS (Problem + Agitate) |
| Solution intro | PAS (Solve) |
| Features blocks | FAB pairs (feature + advantage + benefit, each as one sentence) |
| Testimonials | StoryBrand transformation arcs |
| Pricing | JTBD when category is new, comparative anchoring when established |
| Final CTA / Risk reversal | Bob Stone Gem steps 5–7 |
| Microcopy throughout | Wiebe verbatim from VOC |

## 3 — Five hero formulas (paste-ready)

### Formula 1 — "[X] for [audience]" (Identification)
**When**: B2B SaaS, dev-tools, niche agencies.
**Verbatim**: `Email for developers.` (Resend) · `Financial infrastructure for the internet.` (Stripe) · `Scheduling infrastructure for everyone.` (Cal.com)

### Formula 2 — "[Capability] without [pain]" (Friction elimination)
**When**: prosumer, fintech, B2B services.
**Verbatim**: `Design interfaces without writing code.` (Webflow) · `Build complex apps without backend engineers.` (Supabase) · `Process payroll without the paperwork.` (Gusto)

### Formula 3 — "[Job] faster / better" (Acceleration)
**When**: AI tools, project management, analytics.
**Verbatim**: `Ship products faster.` (Linear, historical) · `Close deals faster.` (HubSpot) · `Write production code faster.` (Cursor)

### Formula 4 — "The [category] that [outcome]" (Category redefinition)
**When**: communication platforms, HR tech, marketing software.
**Verbatim**: `The issue tracking tool you'll enjoy using.` (Linear) · `The web framework for content-driven websites.` (Astro)

### Formula 5 — Imperative command "[Verb modifier]" (Action focus)
**When**: CZ B2B SaaS (plurálový imperativ), B2C consumer apps. The EN imperative version also works (`Think together.` / `Make products that matter.`), but the high-leverage CZ pattern is the plurálový imperativ explicitly.
**CZ verbatim**: `Fakturujte jednoduše.` (Fakturoid) · `Osvoboďte se od papírování.` (iDoklad) · `Dělejte produkty, na kterých záleží.` (Productboard CZ)
**EN verbatim**: `Think together.` (Notion) · `Make products that matter.` (Productboard) · `Ship products faster.` (Linear)

## 5 — Landing-page sequence (canonical SaaS 2026)

1. **Hero** — H1 + subhead + primary CTA + secondary CTA + visual anchor (UI screenshot or short product video)
2. **Social proof strip** — 5–8 customer logos, greyscale, immediately below hero
3. **Problem framing** — PAS Problem + Agitate block, ~3–5 sentences, quantifies pain in time/$
4. **Solution intro** — single-sentence bridge to the platform
5. **Features × Outcomes pairs** — 3–6 cards, each pairs a feature with the business outcome
6. **Testimonials with attribution** — face + full name + role + company + quantified result. Anonymous = 0 value
7. **Objection handling / FAQ** — open answers to the real objections from sales calls
8. **Pricing** (or value anchor for enterprise) — tiered, transparent, with `Most popular` decoy on middle tier
9. **Final CTA + risk reversal** — guarantee / free trial / no credit card required + repeated primary CTA
10. **Footer** — secondary nav + legal

## 6 — Section sequences per business type (deviations)

| Business type | Deviation from canonical |
|---|---|
| **B2C e-commerce** | Remove separate Pricing (price on product card). Add UGC + materials photography directly under hero. CTAs are `Add to cart` / `Buy now`. |
| **Agency / service** | Remove Pricing or replace with `Starting from $X` anchor. Primary CTA becomes `Book a discovery call`. Massively expand Testimonials → deep case studies. |
| **Infoproduct / course** | Fuse Problem + Solution into long-form VSL. Replace Features with course modules list. Add big Risk Reversal (30-day money-back). Add urgency (countdowns, cohort closing). |
| **Marketplace** | Hero has dual CTA (`Find a freelancer` vs `Become a freelancer`). Body emphasizes network liquidity, not software features. |
| **Dev-tool / open-source** | Add code snippet directly to hero. Replace customer logos with GitHub stars, npm downloads, contributors count. CTA splits into `Install` + `Read docs`. |

| See how it works | Podívat se, jak to funguje | Secondary on complex products |
| Book a demo | Domluvit ukázku | Enterprise B2B |
| Get a proposal | Získat cenovou nabídku | B2B services |
| Add to cart | Přidat do košíku | B2C e-commerce (never replace with creative variants) |
| Shop the collection | Prohlédnout kolekci | B2C top-of-funnel |
| Download the report | Stáhnout report | B2B lead magnet |
| Claim my spot | Rezervovat mé místo | Infoproduct / webinar (scarcity) |
| Read the docs | Přečíst dokumentaci | Dev-tool secondary |
| Deploy to Vercel | Nasadit na Vercel | One-click integrations |
| Create an account | Vytvořit účet | Neutral SaaS signup (use sparingly — sounds like work) |
| Try it out | Vyzkoušet si to | Interactive prosumer tools |

**Cardinal CZ rule**: never `Odeslat` for a conversion CTA — it's an abstract verb that kills clicks. Always name the outcome: `Získat přístup`, `Stáhnout e-book`, `Rezervovat termín`.

## 8 — Slogan patterns (20 paste-ready frames)

When the operator asks for a slogan / tagline / social-banner copy, work from this set instead of free-association:

1. `The future of [category] is here.` / `Budoucnost [kategorie] je zde.`
2. `The right [tool] for [niche audience].` / `Správný [nástroj] pro [úzkou cílovku].`

## 9 — Objection-handling templates (LAER framework)

For FAQ blocks + objection callouts. LAER = Listen, Acknowledge, Explore, Respond.

### Price objection (ROI doubt)
> Zdá se vám měsíční poplatek drahý? Rozumíme. Pokud nástroj zkrátí váš [proces] o pouhých [X] hodin týdně, vrátí se investice [v Y měsících / při Z dohodnutých klientech].

### Migration / IT time objection
> Máte oprávněné obavy z přechodu? Vytvořili jsme 1-klikový importér z [konkurence A] a [konkurence B]. Tým může pokračovat v práci bez ztráty dat do 15 minut.

### Technical complexity / training objection
> Bojíte se, že se to vaši zaměstnanci nenaučí? [Produkt] vypadá a chová se jako nástroje, které tým denně používá. Pokud ovládá [obvyklý nástroj], zvládne i toto bez školení.

### Lock-in / long contract objection
> Nenutíme vás do dlouhodobých smluv. Platíte měsíčně, zrušíte kdykoliv jedním kliknutím. Data si při odchodu zdarma exportujete do CSV.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

| Prosumer / dev-tool | FAB | Feature-led | Tech specs + code snippets | GitHub stars, npm downloads, SOC2 | `Install via CLI`, `Read documentation` | Marketing slang ("revolutionary") |

## 11 — A/B-tested data points to lean on (2023–2026)

- **First-person CTAs in EN** (`Start my free trial`, `Get my report`) statistically outperform second-person (`Start your free trial`) for personal-benefit framing (PLoS ONE meta-analysis 2024).
- **CZ caveat**: Czech doesn't carry the first-person CTA effect cleanly — `Spustit mou zkušební verzi` reads as infinitive, not first-person. To reproduce the effect, frame the CTA as a user-spoken sentence: `Chci spustit zkušební verzi` / `Stahuji report`. Use sparingly; the imperative + outcome pattern (`Spustit zkušební verzi`, `Stáhnout report`) is the safer CZ default. Test before deploying.
- **Body copy in second person** (`you` / `vás`) outperforms first-person — opposite direction from CTAs.
- **Odd numbers in headlines** outperform even numbers ~20% (perceived as more authentic).
- **Negative-superlative openers** (`Stop wasting...`, `Never worry about...`) beat positive equivalents up to 30% in CTR — negativity bias + pattern-interrupt against corporate positivism.
- **Form field reduction** 11→4 → 120% conversion lift (Unbounce case study). Top-of-funnel: email only.
- **Annual default toggle on pricing** lifts ARR 15–20%.
- **Decoy effect** ("Most popular" on middle tier) shifts 10–15% of buyers toward the middle tier — raises AOV.
- **CTA repetition** on long pages → 84% lift (GoodUI: 7% → 12.8% conversion).
- **Higher button position** on Airbnb's home page (next to calendar, above price list) shipped after meaningful lift in clicks (GoodUI leak).

## 12 — Harry Dry's 3 filters (apply to every claim)

Before any claim ships, run it through three tests. If it fails any one, rewrite with a concrete metric, a verifiable promise, or a differentiator the competitor can't claim.

1. **Can I visualize it?** Concrete imagery beats abstract concepts.
   - Bad: `Innovative portable music device with high capacity.`
   - Good: `1,000 Songs In Your Pocket.` (Apple iPod)
2. **Can I falsify it?** A claim a reader could check builds credibility.
   - Bad: `Best and fastest delivery in your city.`
   - Good: `Pizza Delivered in 30 Minutes or It's Free.` (Domino's)
3. **Can nobody else say this?** Differentiation = specific method, exact number, or unique proof.
   - Bad: `Trusted by leading companies.`
   - Good: `Used by 4 of the top 5 US banks.`
